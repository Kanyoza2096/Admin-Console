e biggest improvements I’d make are:

1. Build a real Mission Control homepage.
   Today the dashboard is visually strong, but still mostly a topology plus activity feed, and even hardcodes some KPI values in Dashboard.tsx:L200-L220 . Replace that with one operator view: system health, active incidents, workflow queue, connector failures, recent audit events, and live logs.
2. Reorganize navigation around operator jobs, not feature pages.
   The current nav is broad but flat in Layout.tsx:L67-L94 . A premium console should group around Observe , Investigate , Operate , Configure , Govern .
3. Split the Zustand store into domain modules.
   Right now useStore.ts:L180-L260 and useStore.ts:L583-L760 mix auth, transport, polling, Supabase, telemetry, and UI state. For a top-tier console, move to domain slices plus TanStack Query for server state.
4. Add an Incident workflow.
   Your backend already emits and documents events like worker_error , provider_failed , scan_complete , workflow_* in dashboard.py:L232-L246 and socket_server.py:L151-L181 . The frontend should convert those into incident cards with severity, affected services, suggested actions, and linked evidence.
5. Turn topology into drilldown, not decoration.
   The architecture view is useful, but each node should open a service detail page with health, logs, metrics, config, recent failures, and allowed actions. Right now the topology is mostly a visual surface in Dashboard.tsx:L233-L237 .
6. Make workflow operations first-class.
   The backend supports history, pause, resume, trigger, cancel, rollback . That deserves a proper run timeline UI, step progress, blast radius, rollback confirmation, and post-run report.
7. Build a true live log explorer.
   You already have recent logs, SSE stream, stats . Add a log console with filters, pinning, saved searches, and “jump from incident to logs”.
8. Make workspace and tenant hierarchy explicit.
   The backend has workspaces , brands , AI profiles , social accounts , and tenants . The UI should show this as a scoped resource tree, not just selectors.
9. Stop faking “enterprise” where backend is still stubbed.
   A few backend endpoints are placeholders: feature toggle , notifications , tenant switch . Premium consoles are honest: show preview , simulated , or read-only states until the backend becomes authoritative.
10. Add change governance.
    Use the existing audit logs endpoint to build “who changed what, when, and why”, with approval flows for dangerous operations like workflow pause/resume, connector edits, and AI config changes.
11. Make the shell more like a real operator desktop.
    The shell in Layout.tsx:L647-L760 is a good start. Push it further with resizable panes, dockable panels, saved layouts, keyboard-first actions, and persistent operator workspaces.
12. Create an AI Control Room .
    Since the frontend already has typed access to AI config, persona, workflow, guardian, metrics , give operators a dedicated room for model routing, prompt overrides, persona rollout, token burn, and failure fallback.
